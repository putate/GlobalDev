    About the Data

    The data used in this web app is from the CORGIS Dataset Project and can be found here. The following data contains records collected on different countries and geographic locations from 1980 - 2013 from the World Bank. Included is different data about urban development, agriculture and rural development, health, and infrastructure.


    Birth rate is one of the statisitics measured for each country every year. BIRTH RATE is the number of resident live births for a specified geographic area (nation, state, county, etc.) during a specified period (usually a calendar year) divided by the total population (usually mid-year) for that area and multiplied by 1,000.(Source). The death rate is also one of the tracked statistics. Death rate is a measure of the number of deaths (in general, or due to a specific cause) in a particular population, scaled to the size of that population, per unit of time. Mortality rate is typically expressed in units of deaths per 1,000 individuals per year; thus, a mortality rate of 9.5 (out of 1,000) in a population of 1,000 would mean 9.5 deaths per year in that entire population, or 0.95% out of the total.(Source)


    All countries are listed multiple times throughout the json file, this is because each listing of a country contains an entirely different set of data pertaining to the year that it is listed in.
